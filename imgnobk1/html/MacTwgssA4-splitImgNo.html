The other imaginery (complex) number#

If quantum mechanic is about the rotation, one observe is that its magnitude \(|i|\) does not change during rotation. The reason is that its magnitude is :

  \(|x + yi|^2 = (x + yi)(x - yi)\) i.e.   its conjugate multiply or   \(x^{2} + y^{2}\)

which is the formula for the cirlce. The circle radius does not change. The key precisely because the terms multiple, it is

  \(x^2 + xyi -xyi - y^2i^2\) and the \(i^2\) makes the last \(-y^2\) becomes +ve.

It is another dimension from ordinary real number even though in this case it looks more like a real number. And on the real number as well4-1.

also look carefully#

Whilst we have very similar arrangement for its magnitude to be:

Note the negative sign there. This is because now

  \(x^2 + xyj -xyj - y^2j^2\) and the \(j^2\) is +v and hences the \(-y^2\) remain -ve.

Hence, similarly we have rotation involved. But it no longer rotate across a circle. Instead its magnitude is still a constant but draw 2 lines and 4 hyperbola i.e.
