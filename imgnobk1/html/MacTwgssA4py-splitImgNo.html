def plot_vector2d(vector2d, origin=[0, 0], **options):
    return plt.arrow(origin[0], origin[1], vector2d[0], vector2d[1],

def arrow_plots(no_of_arrows, ori, end, color='w'): # mag, color_in='w'):
    x_slice = (end[0] - ori[0]) / float(no_of_arrows) # mag[0] / float(no_of_arrows)
    y_slice = (end[1] - ori[1]) / float(no_of_arrows) # mag[1] / float(no_of_arrows)
    for i in range(no_of_arrows):
        plot_vector2d([             x_slice,              y_slice], 
               origin=[ori[0]+   i *x_slice, ori[1]+   i *y_slice],
                      color=color)

The other imaginery (complex) number#

If quantum mechanic is about the rotation, one observe is that its magnitude \(|i|\) does not change during rotation. The reason is that its magnitude is :

  \(|x + yi|^2 = (x + yi)(x - yi)\) i.e.   its conjugate multiply or   \(x^{2} + y^{2}\)

which is the formula for the cirlce. The circle radius does not change. The key precisely because the terms multiple, it is

  \(x^2 + xyi -xyi - y^2i^2\) and the \(i^2\) makes the last \(-y^2\) becomes +ve.

What if we invent another imaginery number call split complex number where#

  **\( j^{2} = +1!\) **

Well, you may say it is pointless, as j is just 1;#

But that is not true even in ordinary mathematics we talked as j can be \(+/-1\) and not the same as \(1\). That is you cannot exchange \(1\) with \(j\) as you miss \(-1\). It is another dimension from ordinary real number even though in this case it looks more like a real number. And on the real number as well4-1.

also look carefully#

Whilst we have very similar arrangement for its magnitude to be:

  \(|x + yj|^2 = (x + yj)(x - yj)\) i.e.   still its conjugate multiply or \(x+yj\) \(*\) \(x-yj\) but its result is different   \(x^{2} - y^{2}\)

Note the negative sign there. This is because now

  \(x^2 + xyj -xyj - y^2j^2\) and the \(j^2\) is +v and hences the \(-y^2\) remain -ve.

Hence, similarly we have rotation involved. But it no longer rotate across a circle. Instead its magnitude is still a constant but draw 2 lines and 4 hyperbola i.e.

  \( |A|^2 = (a + bj)(a - bj) = a^2 - b^2 \).

The -ve sign totally change thing. Hence it will rotate like a hyperbola and not a circle4-2.
